Muhammad Ali Jinnah

1876 - 1948

Father of the Nation

Quaid-e-Azam Muhammad Ali Jinnah, the founding father of Pakistan, was a pivotal figure in the country’s history. Born on December 25, 1876, Jinnah emerged as a steadfast leader who championed the cause of Muslim nationalism in the Indian subcontinent. His vision and relentless pursuit of a separate homeland for Muslims culminated in the creation of Pakistan in 1947. Known for his profound leadership, legal acumen, and unwavering commitment to his principles, Jinnah's legacy continues to inspire and guide Pakistan as it navigates its path forward.

Biography

  • Joined the Indian National Congress in 1906, advocating for Hindu-Muslim unity and Indian self-rule.
  • Joined the All India Muslim League in 1913, where he initially sought reforms for Muslims within the framework of a united India.
  • By the 1930s, Jinnah shifted his focus towards the idea of a separate nation for Muslims, driven by concerns over their political and cultural future.
  • Served as Pakistan’s first Governor-General from 1947 until his death in 1948.
